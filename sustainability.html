Sustainability through Upcycling

The Role of Upcycling in Sustainability



According to the United Nations Brundland Commision, Sustainability is "meeting the needs of the present without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs". Sustainable Development Goals(SDGs) are the world's plan to achieve a better world for people and planet. SDGs are interconnected and consist of 17 goals and 169 targets which address the global challenges.

Upcycling plays a vital role in achieving Sustainability.

SDG 8:Decent Work and Economic Growth

Simply, Upcycling is the process of transforming waste into a new product with a value. So, it helps to reduce the wastage, reduce the waste management cost and reduce the money spent on buying new products in a household as well as in a country. Upcycling generates new job opportunities and helps to increase the ecocnomic growth in a country.

SDG 12:Responsible Consumption and Production

Upcycling contributes a lot in Responsible Consumption and Production. In the process of Upcycling, waste materials are not broken down into raw materials. Hence, it uses less resources and reduces the production cost than recycling. This concept can be applied in industries like textiles, jewellery, furniture and interior designing.

SDG 15:Life on Land

Most probably, waste matters are transported into wetlands or landfilling sites. It affects all the living and non-living organisms in a bad way. Environmental pollution, spread of diseases, loss of habitats and loss of bio-diversity are some of the harmful effects caused by the improper disposal of garbage. As upcycling helps to reduce the amount of waste thrown into the environment, it helps to minimize the above effects and ensure the lives on land.
